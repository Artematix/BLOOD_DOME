# Economy Rules

## Summary

Gold is an integral part of the Blood Dome. You use it to gain items, consumables, hire mercenaries, and generally survive.

---

## Earning Gold

You gain gold primarily from:
- **Killing enemies** (most drop only a couple gold at most)
- **Completing quests**
- **Murdering each other** (PvP kills)

### Gold Value Reference
| Category | Gold Range |
|----------|------------|
| Cheap items | ~3 gold |
| Expensive items | ~30 gold |
| Most enemy drops | 1-3 gold |

---

## Spending Gold

There are two primary spots to use your gold:

### 1. The Marketplace (Top North)

Jabal's generosity has led to the gathering of a great marketplace in the top north portion of the map.

**Available:**
- Merchants peddling various wares
- Mercenaries for hire (useful in a fight)
- Bounty hunters

### 2. Amith Shrines (Scattered)

Amith, being the god of Wealth and Riches, has shrines scattered around the Blood Dome.

**Amith offers:**
- An extensive shop full of magic and boons
- Rewards for spending gold
- Rewards for accumulating the most wealth

His shop and boons are **extremely powerful and coveted**.

---

## Amith Boons

Spend gold at Amith shrines to gain powerful permanent bonuses:

| Cost | Boon |
|------|------|
| Every 20 gp | +1 AC |
| Every 10 gp | +1 force damage |
| 25 gp | +5 ft. movement per turn |
| 25 gp | +1d4 to saving throws |
| 25 gp | +1d4 to ability checks |
| 50 gp | Active radar for enemies |
| 75 gp | When an event is rolled, teleport up to 60 ft. |
| 100 gp | **Powerword Gold** - Target makes DC 20 CON save or be turned to gold |

---

## Shop Rules

- Buy prices are listed on items
- Sell price is typically **50% of buy price**
- Some items cannot be sold
- Some items are character-exclusive

---

## Item Pricing Guidelines

When creating items, use these benchmarks:

| Rarity | Approximate Price |
|--------|-------------------|
| Common | 3-10 gold |
| Uncommon | 10-20 gold |
| Rare | 20-30 gold |
| Very Rare | 30-50 gold |
| Legendary | 50+ gold |

---

## Notes

- Gold accumulation matters for Amith rewards
- Spending vs. saving is a strategic choice
- The marketplace and Amith shrines offer different benefits
- Consider the economy when balancing new items and quest rewards
